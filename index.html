Current Work

I am currently conducting a research project that focuses on understanding the seasonal and spatial variations in water and sediment quality in the Nueces/Corpus Christi Bay area, Texas.

The project involves sampling water and sediment from five key sites — including Labonte Park, Nueces Bay, Sgt. J.D. Bock Park, Sugar Tree Apartments, and Ennis Joslin Road — during both wet and dry seasons.

Key parameters being measured include:

  • Water quality: pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen, conductivity, salinity, total dissolved solids (TDS), and concentrations of nutrients (nitrate, nitrite, ammonium, phosphate) and heavy metals.
  • Sediment quality: grain size distribution, organic carbon content, total phosphorus, and heavy metals and pesticides.

Preliminary results:

  • pH and salinity levels peaked during the fall, particularly in urban-adjacent sites.
  • Suspended sediment concentrations (SSC) were highest in Nueces Bay, showing nearly double the load between September and November.
  • Nitrate levels were elevated in November while phosphate became undetectable.
  • Sediments shifted from coarser to finer particles, with associated changes in organic carbon content.

Advanced analytical methods including Ion Chromatography, ICP-MS, and Gas Chromatography (pending instrumentation) are used for precise chemical analysis.

I am also preparing peer-reviewed manuscripts and presenting findings at the 2025 Universities Council on Water Resources (UCOWR) Annual Conference in Minneapolis, MN.

This research ultimately aims to support better environmental monitoring and inform coastal watershed management practices in Texas and beyond.
